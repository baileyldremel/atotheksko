Pushing the boundries of what a typeface is

During one of our sessions talking about our inspirations for typefaces, someone brought up this project called ‘Glyph World’, where there are nine different typefaces represented by different ‘landscapes’. This breaks my defenition of a typeface as it’s different styles don’t match. They all look like different typefaces, but they are all the same font. Why is that? I suppose that’s the question the project is asking.
